## 1. Positioning Statement

For SkyLoyal frequent flyers, **Loyalty Rescue** is the proactive retention programme that recognises your loyalty before you have to ask, because it reads the signals (tier slips, dormant miles, drift to partners) and reaches out the way a good travel agent would, with respect, transparency, and a real offer.

## 2. Three Messaging Pillars

**Pillar 1: Proactive Care.** We notice before you leave. Anchored in the Designer's CD2 Development drive (tier-progress ring) and the operational reality that Active Member Ratio is recovered by talking to people *before* they go quiet, not after. Sample line: "We saw you are three segments from Gold. Here are three flights that fit the way you fly."

**Pillar 2: Flyer-First Transparency.** Every contact tells you why you got it. Anchored in CD3 Empowerment + GDPR Article 22. We refuse the "we used science to know you" language. Sample line: "You are seeing this because you are a Gold member with retention communications turned on. Adjust any time in your settings."

**Pillar 3: Rewarded Loyalty, Real Value.** What we send is a real offer, not a teaser. Anchored in CD4 Ownership and CD7 Unpredictability with hard rules: every "surprise" is a real benefit already loaded to the account. Sample line: "Your lounge pass is already in your wallet. No expiry, no conditions."

## 4. Customer Email Sequence

Three emails, one per archetype that we will run in the pilot. Plain text. No countdowns. Each opens with the lawful-basis disclosure in plain English. Tone: human, specific, never corporate.

### 4.1 Email to Nuala (Status-Slipper)

**Subject:** Three flights to retain your Gold

You are receiving this because you are a SkyLoyal Gold member with tier-progress communications turned on, adjust any time in your account settings.

Hi Nuala,

Quick one. You have flown six segments with us this year, three short of what you need to retain Gold by 31 December. Here are three trips on your usual routes that would close the gap, two of them on dates you have flown before:

- Dublin to Boston, 14 November (your usual North-American window)
- Dublin to London, 28 November (we have nine departures that day)
- Dublin to Lisbon, 9 December (long weekend, partner sale on)

If none of these work, your CSM Aoife will call on day 45 with a personal option. No pressure, the call is opt-out from this same email if you would rather not.

See your tier ring and miles balance any time at your <a href="https://pramithirks-dev.github.io/skyloyal/flyer-view.html">My Loyalty page</a>.

Aoife & the SkyLoyal team

### 4.3 Email to Rashida (Complaint-Scarred)

**Subject:** Your bag in Madrid, what we did about it

You are receiving this because you are a SkyLoyal member, and because you had a difficult trip on 14 February. We track that, and it matters.

Hi Rashida,

I am writing to you because your bag was four days late on the Edinburgh-Madrid route in February. I want to tell you what we did. The handler at Madrid was retrained the same week. The interline scan-point that missed your tag has been reconfigured, three other bags would have been delayed by it that month and were not. Your case was the trigger.

You filed an NPS of -50. That is a fair score, and I would have given the same.

There is no offer in this email. The next email from us will give you a choice: 5,000 bonus miles, a lounge voucher, or a £40 charity donation in your name. You pick which one, or none. That choice is yours, and yours alone.

Aoife (your CSM at SkyLoyal)

## 7. Press Release

**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**
**Dublin, 2026-04-29**

**SkyJet Airways pilots agentic AI to spot loyalty lapse before it happens**

SkyJet Airways has begun the closed pilot of Loyalty Rescue, a proactive retention programme that uses an agentic AI organisation to identify frequent flyers at risk of silent churn and to design ethically-grounded interventions. The pilot targets 4,200 Gold-tier members across the Dublin hub for six months from Q3 2026.

The programme replaces SkyLoyal's previous reactive customer-success model, which engaged flyers only after a complaint or cancellation. The new system reads four behavioural signals (tier-slip exposure, miles-balance dormancy, alliance partner-airline leakage, and post-IROP residue) and produces a risk score reviewed by a human Customer Success Manager before any contact. No fully-automated decisions affecting flyers are taken by the model, satisfying GDPR Article 22 protections.

"Our previous model assumed loyalty looked like cancellations. It does not. Loyalty looks like a flyer quietly choosing another carrier for the next trip", said Claire Dubois, SkyLoyal's Chief Customer Officer. "Loyalty Rescue lets us notice that earlier, and reach out the way a good travel agent would, with respect and a real offer."

The flyer-facing experience is anchored in Yu-kai Chou's Octalysis gamification framework, with seven of the eight Core Drives represented. SkyLoyal has deliberately omitted the eighth, CD6 Scarcity, from the pilot, ruling out countdown timers, fake seat counts, and other anxiety-inducing patterns.

The technical platform is documented openly at https://github.com/pramithirks-dev/skyloyal as part of an academic research collaboration with the National College of Ireland.

**Ends.**
